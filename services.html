  1. Governing Rules and Law :  The Dispute will be settled by a Party or the Parties submitting to binding arbitration located in a city located near Lynchburg, Virginia, pursuant to the Comprehensive Arbitration Rules and Procedures of JAMS (“JAMS”), and governed by the laws of the State of Virginia, without regard to its principles of conflict of laws.
  2. Confidentiality : The Parties will maintain the confidential nature of the arbitration proceeding, including any hearing, and the results, and content, unless otherwise required by law or judicial decision.
  3. Arbitrators : A Dispute will be heard by a single arbitrator, selected by the JAM’s default process for selecting arbitrators. The arbitrator will serve as neutral, independent and impartial arbitrator.
  4. Discovery : Each Party agrees to provide the other Party with all documents relevant to the Dispute. There will be no further discovery, including depositions.

  9. Appeal : The Parties will have no right to appeal an award from the arbitrator.
  10. Final Award : Judgment on the award finally rendered by the arbitrator(s) may be entered into a court having jurisdiction thereof.
